Funder-side users · ~8 min read

Before you start

What you're about to learn

The Trade Directory is where a funder builds and works the network of organisations, trade relationships, contracts and invoices behind every lending product. This walkthrough follows one real path through the screens, in the order you'd actually use them.

  • Who it's for Relationship managers and risk officers on the funder side, signing in for the first time.
  • What you'll see Signing in, the organisation directory, one organisation's full trade picture, invoices, contracts, subscriptions, and lending-opportunity prospecting.
  • How to move Use Back / Next below, the step list on the left, or the ← → arrow keys.
Why this order: it mirrors the real dependency chain — an organisation has to exist in the directory before it can hold a relationship, a relationship before a contract references it, and a contract before an invoice draws it into a lending flow.
Step 1 of 9

Step 1 · Sign in

Signing in to your funder account

Every screen after this is scoped to your organisation automatically — you'll only ever see the directory belonging to the funder you sign in as.

sqf.ai/auth/login
  1. 1Email first — the form only shows password and organisation fields after it recognises your email, since one email can belong to more than one organisation.
  2. 2Pick the organisation you're signing in as. If your email is tied to two funder entities, both appear here — pick the one whose directory you need today.
  3. 3Login takes you straight to the directory. There's no separate "dashboard" landing page — the organisation list is the home screen.
Why it matters: the organisation you pick here is the tenant scope for your entire session — every list, every total, every search you do afterwards is filtered to that funder's own directory, never another funder's.

Step 2 · Directory

The organisation directory

Every organisation your funder has ever dealt with lives here — your own onboarded clients, plus their suppliers and buyers, even ones nobody has formally onboarded yet.

sqf.ai/directory
  1. 1Persona chips show every "hat" an organisation wears at once — it can be your Client, someone else's Supplier, and someone else's Buyer, all at the same time. "No persona" means it exists in the directory purely as a reference (e.g. named on an invoice) with no facilitated relationship of its own yet.
  2. 2Search and sort every column — with a large directory, this is how you'll find a specific counterparty fast.
Why it matters: this table is the single source of truth for "who do we know." Before chasing a company by email or spreadsheet, check here first — if they've ever appeared on an invoice or contract, they're already in this list.
Step 3 of 9

Step 3 · Organisation detail

One organisation's full trade picture

Click any row in the directory to open that organisation's own record — its profile, who it trades with, what's under contract, and every invoice it's named on.

sqf.ai/directory/organizations/7
  1. 1Four tabs, one organisation. Overview holds the profile fields (registration number, country, contact); Relationships, Contracts and Invoices each filter down to just this organisation's records — so you never have to cross-reference the global lists by hand.
  2. 2Relationships are directional. Every row names a Supplier side and a Buyer side — "this org" can appear on either side depending on the deal, which is exactly how NURUN shows up twice above: once as buyer, once as supplier.
Why it matters: this is the screen to open before any credit conversation — it's the fastest way to see everything the funder already knows about a counterparty in one place.
Step 4 of 9

Step 4 · Invoice Register

Uploading and tracking invoices

Every invoice financed under any lending product — AR Finance, Supply Chain Finance, Invoice Factoring, Term Loan — lives in one register, not four separate ones.

sqf.ai/directory/invoices
sqf.ai/directory/invoices
  1. 1Pick issuer and debtor from the directory — both must already exist as organisations. If a counterparty genuinely doesn't exist yet, that has to be resolved before the invoice can be filed.
  2. 2Enter line items only — quantity, unit price, tax rate. Never type in a total.
  3. 3Totals are calculated for you from the line items, so a typo in a header total can never drift from what the lines actually say.
Why it matters: because issuer/debtor and the relationship between them are captured automatically from the invoice itself, uploading a new invoice is also how new trade relationships and counterparty personas get recorded — you don't have to set those up separately first.
Step 5 of 9

Step 5 · Contracts

Facility agreements and commercial contracts

Two kinds of contract live side by side here: the funder's own facility agreement with a client, and the commercial contracts between trading counterparties that those facilities finance.

sqf.ai/directory/contracts
  1. 1Facility = the funder is one of the two parties, and a lending product is attached. Commercial = a trade contract between two counterparties, with no funder-lending relationship of its own.
  2. 2Status moves forward only — Draft → Active → Expired/Terminated. A contract has to be made Active before any subscription can reference it as its facility.
Why it matters: a client can't subscribe to a lending product until there's an Active facility contract behind it — this screen is usually the last stop before Subscriptions.
Step 6 of 9

Step 6 · Subscriptions

Which products each client is on

A client can be signed up to more than one lending product at once — this is the map of client → product → the facility contract backing it.

sqf.ai/directory/subscriptions
  1. 1Client persona, not organisation name — this is the client-facing identity (its own generated code) that gets subscribed, since one organisation's Client persona is a distinct facet from its Supplier or Buyer persona.
  2. 2Facility contract links straight back to the Contracts screen — click through if you need to check the facility's limit or end date.
Why it matters: this is the screen risk and relationship teams check to answer "what can this client actually draw on right now" without opening every contract individually.
Step 7 of 9

Step 7 · Opportunities

Finding your next lending opportunity

Everything you've just seen — organisations, relationships, contracts, invoices — also feeds a knowledge graph. This screen turns that graph into ready-made prospecting lists.

sqf.ai/directory/opportunities
  1. 1Saved patterns are pre-built for the three lending products this platform supports today — click Run and the graph returns a ranked candidate list, no query-writing required.
  2. 2Ask the graph is for anything the saved patterns don't cover — type a plain-English question and it's answered against the same live trade data.
Why it matters: this is the payoff for keeping the directory accurate — every invoice and relationship entered earlier in this walkthrough is what makes these prospecting lists trustworthy.
Step 8 of 9

Recap

The whole flow, in one line each

You've now seen the full path from signing in to spotting a new lending opportunity.

  • 1. Sign in scopes your whole session to one funder's directory.
  • 2. Directory is the single list of every organisation the funder has ever dealt with.
  • 3. Organisation detail shows one org's relationships, contracts and invoices together.
  • 4. Invoices are where new counterparties and relationships usually get captured.
  • 5. Contracts hold the facility agreements and commercial terms behind those trades.
  • 6. Subscriptions map each client to the lending products it's actually signed up for.
  • 7. Opportunities turn all of the above into a prospecting list.
Next step: go create a real invoice for a counterparty you know, and follow it through to its own Organisation detail page — the fastest way to make this stick is to do it once for real.
